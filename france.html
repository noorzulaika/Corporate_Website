THINGS TO DO IN FRANCE

Exploring Palace of Versailles

Located outside of Paris, the Palace of Versailles provides a glimpse into the lavish lifestyle of the French aristocracy of King Loius XIV and Marie Antoinette. Guided tours are available and you will want to take advantage of those as there is so much to see, including the Hall of Mirrors, Grand Trianon, the Petit Trianon and the charming rustic Hameau (Hamlet) constructed for Marie Antoinette. One of the most magnificent displays of over-top opulance is the Garden of Versailles with endless ponds, fountains and channels of water.

Enjoy a River Cruise on the Seine

Sightseeing on the Seine River is a unique way to get to know the cities and monuments that exist in France. The Seine runs through the heart of Paris and into the English Channel and is the best way to get a view of the Paris countryside. Cruising the Seine River also means you can marvel at the architecture of the Louvre, the Musee D'Orsay and the Notre Dame Cathedrall from afar. Sightseeing on the Seine can be experienced during the day or on a dinner cruise at night.

Visit the Normandy Region

Spending time in the Normandy area will spark your interest in World War II history, specifically the beaches where Allied forces lannded on D-Day, June 6, 1944, marking the beginning of the end of the war and and victory over the Nazis. Roeun, the capital, is filled with gothic architecture and is where Joan of Arc was tried burned at the stake for heresy in 1431. France's Normandy is worth the day trip.

Learn How to Cook a Gourmet Meal

What better to do in France? Do you love taking food tours around the world? The destinations that is guaranteed to provide a memorable food tour is Paris, France. France is the place that you can learn about its famous cuisines and take some of the knowledge with you. Many classes are offered, from how to make delectable sweets and crepes to preparing traditional gourmet meals and French dishes.

Admire the Eiffel Tower

Constructed in 1889 to mark the 100-year anniversary of the French Revolution, the Eiffel Tower stands more than 1,000 feet tall is named the most-visited landmark in history. The Eiffel Tower is still regarded as an architectural masterpiece and has to become an iconic structure for photos and films from the across the world.
